Get in Touch!

Dr. Mardach is a pediatrician and board certified clinical and biochemical geneticist with interests in the application of genomics to the diagnosis of rare genetic diseases, neurogenetic and metabolic disorders, and newborn screening.  She provides clinical genetic assessments to pediatric and adult patients at the UC Davis Mind Institute Genomics Clinic.

Dr. Mardach is pioneering telehealth genomic education to advance genetic and genomic knowledge among international health providers.  She is also participating in building the Genomics Telemedicine Program that will increase access to specialized care for individuals living in distant geographical areas.

With other Colombian geneticists in the US, she has developed AvanceGen, a work collaboration with Colombian academic and public health institutions, striving to facilitate the implementation of a comprehensive newborn screening program in Colombia. Activities to prepare a pilot newborn screening project in Colombia are currently under way.

Clinical and Biochemical Geneticist

Dr. Mardach provides clinical genetic assessments to pediatric and adult patients at the UC Davis Mind Institute Genomics Clinic. In collaboration with UC Davis Health neurologists, she has begun a neurogenetic service and plans to work with other departments integrating genomics into other medical specialties.

In addition to her work at UC Davis, she is pioneering telehealth genomic education to advance genetic and genomic knowledge among international health providers. She is also participating in building the Genomics Telemedicine Program that will increase access to specialized care for individuals living in distant geographical areas.

Clinical Investigator

As a metabolic specialist, Dr. Mardach has served extensively as an investigator participating in clinical trials for new therapeutic products.
Dr. Mardach was the principal investigator and oversaw all research activities for clinical trials for the approval of new products to treat Fabry, Gaucher and Pompe diseases, disorders of biotin metabolism, urea cycle disorders, and phenylketonuria.

Dr. Mardach procured involvement in unusual clinical cases in pharmaceutical research, resulting in the early application of advanced diagnostic and therapeutic technology to Kaiser Members.

Educator

Medical Professor

Rebecca has taught for 20 years at the David Geffen School of Medicine at UCLA, working with medical, nursing, and genetic counseling students, as well as fellows in biochemical and clinical genetics. She currently teaches at the UC Davis School of Medicine.

Medical Consultant

Dr. Mardach single handedly developed from inception a medical service for the diagnosis and treatment of metabolic patients and founded the innovative Kaiser Regional Metabolic Center in Los Angeles. The center quickly became a model of medical care for chronic, complex, and frequently non-treatable inborn errors of metabolism.

Dr. Mardach also coordinated the newborn screening program at Kaiser. The program covers over 600,000 newborns each year and allows for early diagnosis and treatment of conditions like congenital endocrinopathies, cystic fibrosis, sickle cell disease, thalassemia, congenital hearing loss, and a long list of metabolic conditions.
